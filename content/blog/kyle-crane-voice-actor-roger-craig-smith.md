### Early career and background

Roger Craig Smith didn’t start as a voice actor. He began his career doing stand-up comedy during and after college. His comedy acts included funny voices and characters. These caught the attention of people in the entertainment industry. They asked if he had thought about voiceover work, which sparked his interest.

To improve, Smith took voice acting classes in Burbank, California. He spent a few years meeting people and gaining experience. This helped him land small jobs. One of his first gigs was a nurse training video, earning him [$75](<https://www.gamespot.com/articles/roger-craig-smith-on-becoming-batman/1100-6415740/>). It wasn’t fancy, but it was a start. His first video game job came in 2004 with _Castle Shikigami 2_. There, he learned how to improvise while acting.

Smith’s big break happened after taking an animation class in Los Angeles. An agency noticed him there, helping him start his journey to becoming a famous voice actor.

Smith doesn’t just work in games. He’s voiced anime characters, like in _Naruto_ , and played Curtis Miller in _Resident Evil: Degeneration_. His ability to voice different types of characters makes him a favorite among fans and creators.
