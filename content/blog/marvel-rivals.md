## **Testing and Troubleshooting**

### **Testing the Soundboard for Functionality**

Before you dive into using your Marvel soundboard in Marvel Rival, you need to test it thoroughly. Testing ensures that every sound clip works as intended and enhances your overall experience. Start by playing each audio clip individually. Check for clarity, volume consistency, and timing. If a clip sounds distorted or too quiet, adjust it in Dubbing AI and re-import it into Marvel Rival.

Here’s a simple checklist to guide your testing process:

  * **Play Each Clip** : Ensure every sound plays without delays or glitches.

  * **Check Volume Levels** : Balance the volume across all clips to avoid sudden loud or soft sounds.

  * **Test Button Assignments** : Verify that each button triggers the correct sound.

  * **Simulate Gameplay** : Use the soundboard during a mock gameplay session to see how it performs in real-time.

> **Pro Tip:** Use headphones during testing. They help you catch subtle issues like background noise or uneven audio quality.

If you notice any problems, don’t panic. Small tweaks can make a big difference. For example, if a clip overlaps with another, adjust the timing or reassign the button. Testing might feel repetitive, but it’s a crucial step to ensure your soundboard runs smoothly.

### **Common Issues and Solutions**

Even with careful planning, you might encounter a few hiccups. Don’t worry—most issues have straightforward fixes. Below are some common problems and how to solve them:

  1. **Audio Clips Not Playing**

     * **Cause** : The file format might be incompatible with Marvel Rival.

     * **Solution** : Convert the file to a supported format like MP3 or WAV using Dubbing AI or an online converter.

  2. **Volume Imbalance**

     * **Cause** : Some clips may have been recorded at different levels.

     * **Solution** : Use Dubbing AI’s editing tools to normalize the volume across all clips.

  3. **Button Misalignment**

     * **Cause** : Clips might be assigned to the wrong buttons.

     * **Solution** : Revisit the soundboard layout in Marvel Rival and reassign the buttons correctly.

  4. **Lag or Delay During Playback**

     * **Cause** : Your device might be struggling with processing power.

     * **Solution** : Close unnecessary applications to free up resources. If the issue persists, consider upgrading your hardware.

  5. **Distorted Audio**

     * **Cause** : The original clip might have low quality or excessive effects.

     * **Solution** : Re-record the clip with better settings or reduce the applied effects in Dubbing AI.

> **Quick Tip:** Keep a backup of your soundboard files. If something goes wrong, you can restore the original setup without starting from scratch.
