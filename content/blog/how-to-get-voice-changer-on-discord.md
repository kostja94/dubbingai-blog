### Step 3: Set Up the Voice Changer with Discord

Once installed, connect the **voice changer** to Discord. This lets you change your voice while chatting. Follow these steps:

  1. Open Discord and click **User Settings** (gear icon at the bottom-left).

  2. Go to **Voice & Video Settings** in the menu.

  3. Under **Input Device** , pick the voice changer you installed.

  4. Set **Output Device** to your headphones or speakers.

  5. Adjust input sensitivity and test your mic to check the sound.

  6. Save your settings and join a channel to try your new voice.

> **Tip:** Test your setup before using it live. This avoids problems and ensures your voice sounds great.

Remember, some servers have rules about **voice changers**. Always follow the rules and let others know if you’re using one. This keeps things fun and fair for everyone.

### Step 4: Test and Adjust Your Settings

After setting up your **voice changer** , it’s time to test it. This step helps make sure your voice sounds great on Discord. Testing and adjusting might seem hard, but it’s easy if you follow these steps.

#### Start with a Test Run

First, join a private Discord channel or make a test server. Talk into your mic and listen to your voice with the **voice changer**. If it doesn’t sound right, don’t worry. You can change the settings to fix it.

Here’s what to check during your test:

  * **Pitch and Tone** : Change the pitch to make your voice higher or lower. Try different tone settings to find what sounds best.

  * **Background Noise** : Listen for static or echoes. If you hear noise, adjust the sensitivity in Discord or the software.

  * **Real-Time Changes** : Switch between voice presets while talking. This shows how smoothly the tool changes effects.

> **Tip** : Use headphones to hear your voice clearly and avoid sound feedback.

#### Fine-Tune Your Settings

Once you test your voice, start making changes. Most **voice changers** have tools to improve your sound. Here are some features to try:

  * [**Equalizers**](<https://jolt.richmond.edu/putting-words-in-your-mouth-the-evidentiary-impact-of-emerging-voice-editing-software/>): Adjust frequencies to make your voice deeper or softer. For example, boost low sounds for depth or lower high sounds to reduce sharpness.

  * **Filters** : Remove unwanted sounds with filters. These help clear background noise and improve quality.

  * **Spectrograms** : Some tools show visual graphs of your voice. Use these to spot areas that need fixing, like pitch or volume.

If you want a special effect, like copying a celebrity voice, [write down your settings](<https://www.owenforensicservices.com/american-board-of-recorded-evidence-voice-comparison-standards/>). This helps you use the same sound later.

#### Test Again and Repeat

After adjusting, test your voice again. Keep testing until you’re happy with the sound. Don’t rush—getting it right takes time. If you use advanced tools, like analyzers, use them to fine-tune your settings.

> **Pro Tip** : Save your favorite settings as presets. This makes it easy to switch voices during Discord chats.

By testing and adjusting, your **voice changer** will work perfectly. Whether for fun or privacy, this step ensures the best experience.
