### How Seth Green Joined Family Guy

Have you ever thought about how Seth Green got to voice Chris Griffin in Family Guy? The story is actually pretty funny. When he auditioned, Seth did a [Buffalo Bill impression](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seth_Green>) from _The Silence of the Lambs_. Yes, that creepy guy from the movie! But Seth didn’t just copy the voice. He imagined Buffalo Bill talking through a McDonald’s speaker. This funny idea impressed the show’s creators, and Seth got the role.

Seth Green didn’t only voice Chris Griffin. He also voiced Neil Goldman, another character in the show. Seth’s talent for creating unique voices made him a great choice for the cast. From the start, Seth Green and Chris Griffin were a perfect match. He adds humor and creativity to every episode.

### Why Seth Green’s Chris Griffin Is Special

Seth Green’s Chris Griffin is unique because of his funny voice. Chris has a silly, awkward tone that makes him unforgettable. Seth based this voice on his Buffalo Bill impression but made it funnier to fit Chris’s personality.

What makes Seth’s work even better is how he shows Chris’s innocence. Whether Chris is failing at school, dealing with his odd family, or being a clumsy teen, Seth’s voice makes every moment funny. He mixes comedy with heart, making Chris a lovable character.

Seth Green doesn’t just give Chris a voice—he gives him personality. That’s why Chris Griffin is one of Family Guy’s most famous characters.
