## Creative Process in Hazbin Hotel Season 2

### Visual Style Evolution

You notice the difference in hazbin hotel season 2 as soon as you start watching. The animation feels smoother and more alive. The creative team understands the show better now, so every artist gets a chance to shine. You see more action and movement in every scene. Vivienne Medrano, the creator, said the animation has “really leveled up.” You can spot the changes in the way characters move and interact.

> Vivienne Medrano shared, “With Hazbin Hotel season two, what I’m most excited for people to see is one, the [quality of the show on every level has ‘upped’](<https://comicbook.com/anime/news/hazbin-hotel-creator-season-2-better-than-1/>). I think the writing is stronger, I think the pacing is stronger, and we were given a little bit more time so we have more ability to let the story breathe.”

The cast also feels the change. Amir Talai, who voices Alastor, talked about the pressure in the recording booth. He said, “[I felt a heaviness in the booth](<https://collider.com/hazbin-hotel-season-2-cast-pressure-confidence-character-growth/>). I was like, ‘Get this right. Do it for Viv, do it for the fans.'” Blake Roman, who plays Angel Dust, felt more relaxed this season. He said, “In Season 2… I know that people like this character and like my take on this character. We know that people like the show… Now I get to just go in and have fun.” Erika Henningsen, who voices Charlie Morningstar, hinted that Charlie’s story might surprise fans. She said, “I think our fans might get a little… upset with her. But Viv has this master plan, and every character has to go through growth.”
