#### The artistry behind Homer’s voice and catchphrases

Dan’s journey to becoming the voice of Homer is fascinating. Initially, he [based Homer’s voice on Walter Matthau](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dan_Castellaneta>), but he found it hard to sustain during long recording sessions. So, he adapted by lowering his chin and letting his IQ “drop,” creating the hilarious and lovable Homer we know today. Dan’s commitment to the role is unmatched. He stays in character during recordings, visualizing scenes to deliver the perfect tone. This dedication has given us unforgettable catchphrases like “D’oh!” and “Woo-hoo!”

## The Evolution of Voice Acting in The Simpsons

![The Evolution of Voice Acting in The Simpsons](https://dubbingai.io/blog2/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/d0687ac5ea1848548fbcb9b2b1d05dd5.webp)Image Source: [pexels](<https://pexels.com>)

### Changes in The Simpsons Cast

#### Recasting of characters and reasons behind it

Over the years, some changes have reshaped the cast of _The Simpsons_. Pamela Hayden, who voiced Milhouse, retired after [nearly 700 episodes](<https://www.unilad.com/film-and-tv/news/simpsons-characters-replaced-pamela-hayen-milhouse-463980-20241107>), leading to a recasting of the character. Hank Azaria stepped back from voicing Apu, citing concerns about representation and stating that it “didn’t feel right” anymore. To ensure authenticity, Tony Rodriguez, a Cuban actor, now voices Julio. Similarly, Dawnn Lewis replaced Tress MacNeille as Bernice Hibbert, reflecting a growing commitment to diversity in voice representation. These changes highlight how _The Simpsons_ adapts to evolving cultural and industry expectations while staying true to its roots.

#### Adapting to cultural and industry shifts

The entertainment industry has shifted significantly, and _The Simpsons_ has kept pace. The show has embraced inclusivity, ensuring characters are voiced by actors who share their cultural backgrounds. This approach not only enriches the storytelling but also resonates with modern audiences. By evolving with the times, _The Simpsons_ continues to set an example for other animated shows.

### Technological Advancements in Voice Acting

#### Impact of new recording techniques on the show

Technology has revolutionized voice acting, and _The Simpsons_ has embraced these advancements. Modern recording techniques allow voice actors to deliver performances with greater precision and flexibility. Remote recording setups have also become common, enabling the cast to work from different locations without compromising quality. These innovations ensure the show maintains its high standards while adapting to new workflows.

#### The role of AI in the future of voice acting

Artificial intelligence is making waves in the world of voice acting. Some voice actors have expressed [concerns about AI replicating their voices](<https://wccftech.com/the-simpsons-voice-actor-believes-ai-will-replace-his-work/>). One actor shared, “I imagine that soon enough, artificial intelligence will be able to recreate the sounds of the [more than 100 voices](<https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2025/02/04/opinion/simpsons-hank-azaria-voice-acting-AI.html>) I created for characters on _The Simpsons_ over almost four decades. It makes me sad to think about it.” While AI can mimic voices, it lacks the emotional depth and creativity that human actors bring to their roles. As one actor put it, “A misconception about voice acting is that it takes only a voice. But our bodies and souls are involved to get the proper believability.”
