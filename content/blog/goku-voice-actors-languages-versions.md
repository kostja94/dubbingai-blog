## Goku’s Journey in Dragon Ball and Pop Culture

### The Origins of Son Goku

Son Goku, the hero of Dragon Ball, has deep roots. His character is inspired by Sun Wukong, the Monkey King from _Journey to the West_. This explains Goku’s playful side and love for adventure. But Goku also has modern influences. His fighting style and humor come from martial arts stars like Jackie Chan and Bruce Lee.

Goku’s story starts when he is sent to Earth as a baby. His Saiyan name is Kakarot, and he was meant to take over the planet. A head injury as a child changed him, making him kind and cheerful. Raised by his grandpa, Son Gohan, Goku learned to value friendship and fairness. These traits define him throughout the series.

### Goku’s Role as a Global Icon

Goku is famous far beyond anime fans. As Dragon Ball’s main character, he is known worldwide. In the early 2000s, his fame grew even more. For example, in 2001, Goku was the most searched name on Lycos, with 4.7 million daily hits. This shows how much people love him.

Goku’s influence goes beyond numbers. He inspires fans and creators everywhere. Tennis star Rafael Nadal said Goku inspired him as a kid. Anime creators like Eiichiro Oda (_One Piece_) and Masashi Kishimoto (_Naruto_) were also influenced by Goku. His positive attitude and never-give-up spirit connect with people globally. Goku even appears at events like the Brazilian Carnival and inspires music, like the German band Son Goku.

Goku has won many awards over the years. He ranked high in polls like _Anime Grand Prix_ and _Weekly Shōnen Jump_. In 2007, he was [voted the strongest manga character in an Oricon survey](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goku>). These honors show Goku’s lasting impact as a symbol of hope and strength.

## Masako Nozawa: The First Voice of Goku

![](https://dubbingai.io/blog2/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/5c4f1f35bf7e492b8c42e35248167d94.jpg)

### Her Work in the Japanese Dragon Ball Series

If you’ve watched Dragon Ball in Japanese, you know [Masako Nozawa’s voice](<https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/encyclopedia/people.php?id=508>). She has voiced Goku since the series began, showing her dedication. She started with the original Dragon Ball, voicing young Goku. Over time, she continued in Dragon Ball Z, Super, and Z Kai. She also voiced other Saiyan males in Goku’s family, like Gohan and Goten.

Nozawa’s work earned her awards and recognition in anime. Her voice acting helped Dragon Ball Z become hugely successful. By 1999, it made [$3 billion in Asia](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dragon_Ball_Z>). In North America, it sold 14 million videos by 2002 and 25 million DVDs by 2012. These numbers show how popular Dragon Ball is, with Nozawa’s voice being a big part of its success.

### How Masako Nozawa Shaped Goku’s Personality

Masako Nozawa didn’t just voice Goku; she shaped who he is. She chose not to read the manga, reacting naturally to scripts. This made Goku’s curious and innocent nature feel real. She understood Goku’s growth from a playful kid to a strong hero.

Fans say Nozawa made Goku unforgettable. Her voice showed his cheerful, determined, and adventurous side. By voicing Goku in all Dragon Ball media, she kept his character consistent. Her work inspires other Goku voice actors worldwide, setting a high standard for the role.

This effort kept Goku’s voice steady and real, even in tough scenes. Fans love his work on big moments like the Kamehameha and Super Saiyan scenes.
